Skin Thickness

Skin thickening is frequently observed in patients with diabetes. Affected areas of skin can appear thickened, waxy, or edematous. Clinical evidence of skin thickening (diabetic thick skin) was found in 22% of patients with insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus and in 4% of control subjects (p less than 0.05) so if it more than it, it's still normal.
